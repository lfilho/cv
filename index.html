DataEasy believed in me to be their Software Development Manager and I'm happy to have boosted their software quality, development process and the overall culture of the company.

I started bringing a mixture of agile methodologies (Scrum, XP, Kanban) to the team (12 people) and accumulating some functions of Scrum Master, Product Owner, Tech Leader, DevOps Leader and meeting Driver. Within time, after some training and better knowing each team member, I started delegating this functions and focusing on other managerial tasks.

I have brought "one-on-one" meetings where I could, every other week, closely follow each team member development and satisfaction as well align theirs goals with the company's and exchange feedback. This personal and constant interaction keeps me grounded and real on my plans for the team, as it gives me confidence to carry on based on the positive feedbacks and an early opportunity to adapt and shift directions otherwise.

I could introduce DataEasy to a more collaborative and innovative and relaxed culture by establishing "Research & Development" day: a day where it would be free from the day-to-day work to search for new solutions, new technologies, invest time in side projects... Anything that would potentially add value to the company, our product, our environment or our customers.

The team was also happy with the sporadic Talks I started motivating and facilitating amongst them. Whenever someone would learn a new technology or anything worth sharing, they could schedule a mini-talk (a "brown bag session") for the whole tech team and share the knowledge away.

On the technical side, I happily shook their grounds: introduced SemVer to our conventions; migrated all codebases to Git; adopted Bitbucket (and then GitHub); introduced Slack as the official company communication tool; Google Apps for Business; the great New Relic for monitoring our servers and applications; introduced Maven, automated tests and asynchronous code review to team, rocketing the team's and product's quality. I have also introduced the concept of DevOps and Continuous Integration with some Node.js integrations, Jenkins, Shell Script and Docker containers.

So far, I'm focusing on bettering the development processes through BPM. I've mapped our AS-IS processes and am now working on the MUST-BE and later on the SHOULD-BE versions.
